COVID-19 EFFECTS ON

UNIVERSITY ATTENDANCE

This website would like to share with you the effects of the COVID-19 pandemic on attendance in UK universities. It highlights a comparison and insights associated with the pandemic and university attendance before, during, and after the pandemic (between 2020 and 2024). A comparative perspective will also account for patterns in Pakistan.

The COVID-19 pandemic adversely affected various parts of learning activities in schools globally. The notable aspect is the effects on a typical study program. Following UK government interventions to contain the virus spread by reducing contact and closeness in public spaces, schools embraced virtual learning via different digital platforms. The method proved effective despite accompanying issues such as disparities in access to learning resources in regions with limited supportive educational resources. A US-based study revealed a statistically significant negative relationship between the pandemic and college enrolment for all universities in Texas (Korotchenko and Dobbs, 2023, p. 1). However, other empirical evidence shows that a shift from campus-based lectures to digital ones because of pandemic guidelines showed a significantly higher attendance rate, thus suggesting the effectiveness of online learning (Haugom, 2022, p. 1)

Timeline of University Attendance in the UK

    Before COVID-19 (pre-2020):

  • Standard academic calendar with in-person lectures, seminars, and exams.
  • Traditional application process with UCAS (Universities and Colleges Admissions Service).
  • Physical campus tours and open days for prospective students.
  • Student accommodations filled to capacity with shared living spaces.

    During COVID-19

  • Universities shifted to online learning in response to lockdowns and social distancing measures
  • Remote lectures, seminars, and assessments conducted via virtual platforms
  • Limited campus access with strict health and safety protocols for essential activities.
  • Some students had challenges with internet connectivity and access to technology.

    After COVID-19

  • Gradual return to in-person teaching while maintaining elements of online learning.
  • Schools introduced hybrid learning models to accommodate different learning preferences.
  • University management continued emphasising health and safety measures on campus.
  • Enhanced digital infrastructure and support for remote learning technologies.

Timeline of University Attendance in Pakistan

    Before COVID-19 (pre-2020):

  • The academic year typically follows a traditional semester system.
  • In-person lectures, labs, and exams conducted on campus.
  • The admission process involves the submission of physical documents and in-person interviews.
  • Limited availability of online learning resources and digital infrastructure.

    During COVID-19

  • Universities transitioned to online learning platforms to continue education.
  • Challenges with internet connectivity and access to technology, particularly in rural areas.
  • Delayed academic calendars and postponed examinations due to lockdowns.
  • Schools adopted remote teaching methods, including video lectures and virtual labs

    After COVID-19

  • Integration of hybrid learning models combining online and in-person instruction.
  • Improved digital infrastructure and accessibility to online resources.
  • Continued emphasis on flexible learning options to accommodate diverse student needs.
  • Streamlined admission processes with increased use of online platforms.

Comparison

    UK Impact

  • Swift transitioned to online learning in 2020, followed by a cautious return to in-person classes in 2021.
  • Hybrid teaching models were introduced in 2022 to balance in-person and online education.
  • Vaccine rollout enabled the full reopening of universities by 2023, focusing on student well-being and academic support.
  • Vaccine passports and testing requirements facilitated safe campus activities and accommodation access by 2024.

    Pakistan Impact

  • Digital infrastructure challenges hindered a similar shift to online learning in 2020.
  • Slow vaccination progress prolonged reliance on remote education through 2021 and 2022.
  • In-person classes will gradually resume by 2023 and be supported by improved vaccine distribution efforts.
  • Full reopening of universities in 2024, emphasising blended learning and addressing learning disparities.
